Undertreatment in Real-World Settings Can Lead to Suboptimal Gains in Vision[2-15]

  • There is a huge burden for treatment visits
  • There is a need for better optimized patient outcomes, such as a drug that is longer lasting, that would be more efficacious of drying the macula
  • Real-world registries didn't achieve the similar results as in the pivotal large-scale prospective randomized trials
  • Injection frequency is lower in the real world (real-world evidence studies, registries) compared to clinical trials
<<insert slide 6; 03:45>>

Real-World Evidence Shows that Over 70% of Patients with nAMD are Undertreated in the First Year of Therapy[11,15]

  • Full funding in Australia delivered outcomes close to clinical trials
  • Within Europe, a different funding system resulted in differences in outcome
  • Fewer injections resulted in worse outcomes
  • Neovascular age-related macular degeneration (nAMD) outcomes in China and South America have been affected negatively by reimbursement issues for anti-vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF) agents

  • More injections are associated with better results

The Number of Injections Affects VA Results[17]

  • There are 3 different silos of opportunity:
    • Provide patients initially with better vision
    • Have a greater durability of the treatment
    • Have a long-lasting effect
<<insert slide 9; 06:02>>

Objectives of the Treatment[1]

  • The treatment objective is to:
    • Improve anatomical outcomes
    • Dry the retina
    • Minimize retinal fluid (to improve vision clinically)
    • Then maintain results with less frequent treatment regimens
<<insert slide 10; 07:01>>

Real-World Evidence Highlights the Relationship Between Injection Frequency and VA Gains[18-19]

  • The next question is to evaluate if it is possible to stop the treatment in some patients
  • More research on that topic is needed
  • The recurrence rate is at least 50% in the first year (when treatment is stopped)
<<insert slide 11; 07:37>>

Sustain Treatment: Adequate and Continued Anti-VEGF Therapy Can Maintain Long-Term Vision in the Real World[15-16]

  • The majority of patients need long-term treatment
  • We have no predictive markers to tell in a given patient whether or not to stop treatment, or if they will recur 6 or 12 months later
  • Studies in which patients do well -- and there is some remaining fluid -- were the end result of trials attempting to do everything possible to eradicate the fluid

Emerging Treatments for nAMD

<<insert slide 13; 10:18>>

Molecular Sizes of Anti-VEGF Agents[20-25]

  • Brolucizumab was designed to achieve longer durability, with less injection frequency, through a concentrated molar dosing enabled by a smaller molecular size
  • Brolucizumab is the most advanced single-chain antibody variable fragment developed for ophthalmic use
<<insert slide 14; 11:20>>

HAWK and HARRIER Studies: Brolucizumab Reduced Dosing Frequency[26]

  • The aim was to achieve a longer-term effect in the eye and to be potentially more efficacious
<<insert slide 15; 11:40>>

Patients treated with brolucizumab 6 mg had fewer visits in which sub-RPE fluid was observed from Week 36 to 48 [25]

  • When examining the optical coherence tomography (OCT), and the fluid that is intraretinal and/or subretinal as well as sub-retinal pigment epithelium (RPE), there was an approximately 30% greater reduction in the OCT with brolucizumab compared to aflibercept
  • What those results suggest is that, given the molecular structure of the drug, brolucizumab has the potential to anatomically work more favorable versus aflibercept
<<insert slide 16; 12:27>>

Conbercept for Choroidal Neovascularization Secondary to nAMD: The PHOENIX Study[27]

  • Conbercept is a large recombinant fusion protein with an anti-VEGF activity
  • The PHOENIX trial demonstrated a significant improvement of visual acuity (VA) on conbercept in Chinese nAMD patients
<<insert slide 17; 12:41>>

Designed Ankyrin Repeat Protein (DARPin®): Abicipar Pegol[1]

  • Abicipar pegol could potentially allow for 12 weeks of retreatment cycles
  • Abicipar pegol led to a high inflammation rate of about 15%

Phase 2 Studies Using RG7716[28-30]

  • RG7716 is a bi-specific molecule with 2 fragment antigen-binding (Fab) arms
  • One Fab arm is an anti-VEGF and the other is an anti-angiotensin (Ang)-2, with a modified fragment crystallizable (Fc) portion

OPT-302 in Phase 2B nAMD[31]

  • OPT-302 suppresses VEGF-C and VEGF-D to allow a pan-VEGF suppression

Phase 3 CEDAR and SEQUOIA[33]

  • Abicipar is a 36 kDa molecule
  • The problem with abicipar is inflammation
  • A majority of patients could be early extended to injections every 12 weeks
  • The phase 3 studies -- CEDAR and SEQUOIA -- indicate that patients were stabilized, but more information on patient VA evolution is needed
  • In both studies, there was a 15% inflammation rate
  • Safety is key in this area of treatment, especially with long-term treatment over 10 or 20 years with repeated injections
<<insert slide 24; 19:38>>

Conbercept for Choroidal Neovascularization Secondary to nAMD: The PHOENIX Study[27]

  • The reported incidence of inflammation with conbercept is not high
  • Conbercept, a fusion protein, has great binding capacity, but did not show improved efficacy over other available agents Phase 3 trials are now starting in the United States and Europe
<<insert slide 25; 21:35>>

Targeting Key Angiogenic Pathways With RG7716[34]

  • RG7716 is an anti-Ang-2/anti-VEGF dual molecule that has 2 Fab segments that combat the 2 parts of the pathological process Hypothetically, there is an interplay between VEGF-A and Ang-2
  • The Fc portion has 6 mutations and has been optimized to decrease the amount of systemic exposure and inflammation
<<insert slide 26; 23:43>>

RG7716 in the BOULEVARD Study[35]

  • The BOULEVARD study included patients with diabetic macular edema (DME) and showed very good results on macular edema
<<insert slide 27; 23:53>>

Phase 2 Studies Using RG7716[28-30]

  • The AVENUE study included patients with nAMD and showed the same results as with ranibizumab
  • The STAIRWAY study included patients with nAMD and showed that 65% of patients have been extended to intervals of every 16 weeks
  • The promising part of RG7716 is that it works via the tyrosine kinase receptor and integrins
<<insert slide 28; 24:50>>

Phase 1/2a Data of OPT-302 in nAMD[36]

  • OPT-302 is a pan-VEGF inhibitor
  • Other agents that suppress VEGF-A are associated with an upregulation of VEGF-C and VEGF-D
  • This upregulation may add to the resistance to VEGF-A mono-suppression -- which has been described in cancers (such as lung and colon cancer)
  • In a 3-month study, OPT-302 showed that half of the patients had undetectable neovascular membranes
<<insert slide 29; 26:12>>

Gene Therapy: The Example of RGX-314[37]

  • Most drugs and approaches using gene therapy in ocular and systemic disease have failed
  • The eye is the ideal organ on which to perform gene therapy and there have been successes in monogenetic rare retinal dystrophies
  • nAMD is a highly-attractive pathology for gene therapy
  • The idea behind gene therapy in nAMD is to trigger protein production from cells that mimic an anti-VEGF effect rather than aiming to replace a faulty gene
  • RGX-314 uses the AAV8 virus, which specifically delivers the gene to the retina
  • RGX-314 is an anti-VEGF Fab
