Excessive Daytime Sleepiness in OSA[1,2]

  • Trying to understand why these patients remain sleepy or why there is such a strong association with excessive sleepiness is a major problem
    • <<insert slide 6; Chapter Title: Arousal Threshold and Inflammation; Timecode (00:02:42). >>

    Association of the Arousal Threshold with Inflammation and Sleepiness[3]

  • Waking up too easily to minor airway narrowing (low respiratory arousal threshold [ArTH]) is a key contributor to obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) pathophysiology
  • The extent to which a low ArTH phenotype contributes to daytime sleepiness and inflammation-related clinical sequelae in OSA is unclear
  • The aim of this study was to determine whether the ArTH correlated either with inflammatory markers or daytime sleepiness
    • <<insert slide 7; Timecode (00:03:26). >>

    Arousal Threshold and Inflammation Study: Results[3]

  • When the study was adjusted:
    • The relationship between the ArTH and that of the inflammatory marker C-reactive protein (CRP) disappeared
    • There was no relationship between the ArTH and the sleepiness level
    • <<insert slide 8; Arousal Threshold and Depression; Timecode (00:06:05). >>

    Depression and Arousal Threshold[4]

  • In patients with depression, sleep can be disrupted and fragmented
  • Sleep disruption could be contributing to the ArTH

    Solriamfetol[6,7]

  • Solriamfetol affects both norepinephrine and dopamine
  • Most medications used for narcolepsy affect only dopamine
  • Both neurotransmitters are very important in the control of sleep and wakefulness
    • Increasing dopamine has an effect on improving alertness
    • Norepinephrine may have an effect on inhibiting sleep drive
    • <<insert slide 3; Timecode (00:01:44). >>

    Solriamfetol: Treatment of ES in Narcoleptic Patients With and Without Cataplexy[7]

  • 12-week, placebo-controlled study
  • Assessed main measures for sleepiness

    Upper Airway Stimulation Therapy[10]

  • This therapy is directed at the anatomic factors causing OSA using technology and a device
  • It uses nerve stimulation to open the airway during sleep

    Upper Airway Stimulation

  • Implanted device is comprised of a stimulator implanted on the hypoglossal nerve and a sensor implanted in the chest
  • The sensor detects breathing and sends an electrical current to the nerve when the airway needs to open during sleep

    Diagnostic Challenges in Childhood Narcolepsy[17]

  • Diagnostic delays are very common in children and adolescents with narcolepsy
  • It can take many years for the disease to be recognized and diagnosed
  • This study looked for additional biomarkers in the polysomnogram that could help improve diagnostic accuracy and determine the epidemiology of pediatric narcolepsy
    • <<insert slide 3; Chapter Title: Biomarkers ; Timecode (00:02:35). >>

    REM Sleep[18,19]

  • In normal rapid eye movement (REM) sleep, there is almost no muscle tone, ie, REM atonia
  • Children and adults with narcolepsy have increased muscle tone during REM sleep, ie, REM without atonia (nRWA)
  • This study looked at whether nRWA could serve as a biomarker of narcolepsy
    • <<insert slide 4; Timecode (00:03:16). >>

    Diagnostic Biomarker: nRWA[18]

  • This study used the American Academy of Sleep Medicine (AASM) criteria to score nRWA
    • <<insert slide 5; Timecode (00:04:25). >>

    Diagnostic Biomarker: nRWA Results[18]

  • Data from patients with narcolepsy type 1 and type 2 were combined to determine whether nRWA was a significant biomarker that separated them from other groups of patients
    • <<insert slide 6; Chapter Title: Pediatric Disruptive Nighttime Sleep; Timecode (00:05:38). >>
