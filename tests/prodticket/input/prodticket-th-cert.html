Activity Overview

(event description – 150 words maximum)

The role of aspirin in the secondary prevention of cardiovascular (CV) events has been well established over a long period of time and confirmed by a large number of clinical trials. Recent clinical trial results have added some insights -- and confusion -- over the safety and efficacy of aspirin in the setting of primary prevention. Drs Bhatt, Angiolillo, Chan, and Vega review the latest thoughts on how to identify patients who may benefit from aspirin therapy, the importance of individualizing treatment, and decision sharing with patients.

Left-side Content

Target Audience

(use the applicable statement)

This activity is intended for primary care physicians, cardiologists, and emergency medicine physicians.

Goal Statement

The goal of this activity is to provide guidance on effective application of the most recent antiplatelet therapy data in order to improve patient treatment and outcomes.

Learning Objectives Learning objectives are needed for registration page, print materials, and certificate page.

Upon completion of this activity, participants will:

Have increased knowledge regarding the

Risk factors for cardiovascular disease (CVD) in the primary care setting

Latest clinical data supporting the use of aspirin in cardioprevention

Have greater competence related to

Patient selection for aspirin therapy in the primary care setting 

Tailoring antiplatelet therapy for patients in primary care in order to optimize cardioprevention
