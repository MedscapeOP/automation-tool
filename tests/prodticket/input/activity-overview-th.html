Cervical cancer affects more than a half million women each year worldwide, killing half of those, as 1 woman dies of cervical cancer every 2 minutes. But there is good news. Not only is it possible to dramatically reduce the incidence of cervical cancer, some countries are on track to eliminate cervical cancer through human papillomavirus (HPV) vaccination and cervical screening programs. In this session, pioneers of these efforts from low- and high-resource countries will share global perspectives on challenges, solutions, successes, valuable lessons learned from setbacks and failures, and new targets for reducing HPV-related disease and death.
