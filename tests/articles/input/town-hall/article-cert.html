Activity Overview

(event description – 150 words maximum)

Glaucoma affects millions of people worldwide. Without treatment, glaucoma can result in blindness, and some patients who do receive treatment will also lose their eyesight. The only treatable risk factor for glaucoma is elevated intraocular pressure (IOP); consequently, treatment aims to lower IOP through improvement in aqueous outflow and/or reduction in the production of aqueous humor, either through topical medications, laser therapy, or surgery. We invite you to engage with renowned faculty in this interactive, case-based journey on the management of glaucoma. You will have the opportunity to review and discuss therapeutic options within a range of patient presentations that mirror clinical practice. Expert-led presentations and discussions will reflect on the pathophysiology of glaucoma, first-line treatment options, and personalizing pharmaceutical and surgical approaches in the management of patients with glaucoma.

Left-side Content

Target Audience

(use the applicable statement)

This activity is intended for glaucoma specialists, comprehensive ophthalmologists, and other healthcare providers interested in glaucoma management. 

Goal Statement

The goal of this activity is to enhance physicians' knowledge of pharmaceutical and surgical approaches to glaucoma treatment, as well as selecting and tailoring individualized glaucoma treatment based on patient characteristics.

Learning Objectives Learning objectives are needed for registration page, print materials, and certificate page.

Upon completion of this activity, participants will:

Have increased knowledge regarding the

• Safety and efficacy of pharmaceutical and surgical (minimally invasive glaucoma surgery [MIGS], lasers, etc) approaches to glaucoma treatment

• Similarities and differences in mechanisms of surgical and nonsurgical approaches to glaucoma treatment

Have greater competence related to

• Selecting first-line treatment strategies for patients with glaucoma 

• Selecting an intraocular pressure (IOP)-lowering strategy for patients who have not adequately responded to initial treatment

Demonstrate greater confidence in their ability to

• Tailor glaucoma treatment based on individual patient characteristics
